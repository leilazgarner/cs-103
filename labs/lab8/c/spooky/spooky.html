An Ode to the History of Halloween

Over 2,000 years ago, on Samhain's eve,
When spirits roam, and mortals grieve,
The ancient Celts would light their bonfires,
To ward off evil fears and dark desires.

Through centuries, traditions start to blend,
Christian saints declared All Saint's Day began,
In medieval times, "Souling" went from door to door,
And Scottish and Irish immigrants, brought their lore,

Today, costumes donned and pumpkins carved,
Trick-or-treaters roam for candy starved,
As October draws more near,
So is a night of laughter, tinged with fear.
