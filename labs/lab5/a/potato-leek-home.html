Potato Leek Soup

Why Potato Leek Soup is My Favorite:

Potato leek soup holds a special place in my heart because it's a dish that connects me to cherished memories of my childhood. My mom used to make it for me, and the aroma of leeks and potatoes simmering on the stove instantly transports me back to those y comforting moments. The simplicity of the ingredients and the soothing, creamy texture make it a y nostalgic and timeless comfort food. The way my mom used to serve it, piping hot with a sprinkle of chives on top, made every bowl a warm embrace. Even as an adult, a spoonful of this soup takes me back to simpler times, creating a sense of y warmth and security that is hard to replicate with any other dish.

Ingredients

  • 4 leeks, cleaned and sliced
  • 4 potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled and smashed
  • 2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and roughly chopped into ½-inch pieces
  • 7 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Chives, finely chopped, for serving

Preparation

  1. In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat. Add leeks, onions, and garlic. Sauté until softened.
  2. Add potatoes, broth, water, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until potatoes are tender.
  3. Remove the bay leaf. Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth. Alternatively, transfer batches to a blender.
  4. If desired, stir in milk or cream for a creamier consistency. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh chives if desired.
